Scorpions

German Rock Group

  • 1965 - Formed by a guitarist Rudolf Schenker
  • 1967 - Karl Heinz Vollmer left the band in because of his military obligations, then he could not associate anymore with the concerts and the military life.
  • 1970 - Things began to come together when Schenker's younger brother Michael and vocalist Klaus Meine, who had played together in the Led Zeppelin and Taste cover band Copernicus, joined the band.
  • 1972 - They won a music contest and recorded two songs for a single that was never released on the CCA label, but the songs, "Action" and "I'm Going Mad" were later released on different compilation albums including Psychedelic Gems 2.
  • 1972 - The group recorded and released their debut album Lonesome Crow, with Lothar Heimberg on bass and Wolfgang Dziony on drums and re-recorded versions of their CCA songs.
  • 1974 - The new line-up released Fly to the Rainbow. The album proved to be more successful than Lonesome Crow and songs such as "Speedy's Coming" and the title track established the band's sound.
  • 1982 - Blackout was released and quickly became the band's best selling album to date, eventually going platinum.
  • 1983 - The Scorpions performed to over 375,000 fans on Day 2 at the three-day US Festival concert held in San Bernardino, California during Memorial Day
  • 1985 - The band toured extensively behind Love at First Sting and released their second live album, World Wide Live.
  • 1988 - On the Savage Amusement tour in 1988, the Scorpions became only the second Western group (not American) to play in the Soviet Union.
  • 1993 - The Scorpions released Face the Heat. Bass was handled by Ralph Rieckermann
  • 2015 - 2017 50th anniversary and Return to Forever
